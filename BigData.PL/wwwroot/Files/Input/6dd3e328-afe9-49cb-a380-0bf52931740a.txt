Karl W  Steinbuch  June          in Stuttgart Bad Cannstatt   June         in Ettlingen  was a German computer scientist   cyberneticist  and electrical engineer   He was an early and influential researcher in German computer science  and was the developer of the Lernmatrix  an early implementation of artificial neural networks  Steinbuch also wrote about the societal implications of modern media 


Biography edit 
Steinbuch studied at the University of Stuttgart and in      he received his PhD in physics  In      he joined Standard Elektrik Lorenz  SEL  part of the ITT group  in Stuttgart  as a computer design engineer and later as a director of research and development  where he filed more than    patents      citation needed      Steinbuch completed the first European fully transistorized computer  the ER    marketed by SEL             In      he became professor and director of the Institute of Technology for information processing  ITIV  of the University of Karlsruhe  where he retired in      
In      he began publishing books  in which he tried to influence German education policy  Together with books from colleagues like Jean Ziegler from Switzerland  Eric J  Hobsbawm from the UK  and John Naisbitt his books     which       predicted what he regarded as the coming education disaster of the emerging civic lobby society      citation needed     
In       together with Helmut Gr ttrup  Steinbuch coined the term Informatik  the German word for computer science                        which gave informatics  and the term kybernetische Anthropologie
