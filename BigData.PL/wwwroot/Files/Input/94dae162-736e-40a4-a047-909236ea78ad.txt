							After a      that saw him win many medals  prizes  and championships  the Egyptian judo champion Ramadan Darwish has qualified for the      Rio Summer Olympics 
In the      Judo Grand Prix  held in the German city of D sseldorf from    to    February  Darwish won second place  with Japan       s Haga Ryunosuke coming in first 
In the      Judo Grand Slam  held in Baku  Azerbaijan  from   to    May  Darwish won bronze  Gold and silver went to Russian Adlan Bisultanov and Azeri Elmar Gasimov  respectively 
His victories throughout      include winning the      Judo Grand Prix  held in the Hungarian capital of Budapest  on    and    June  In the final he won against the Greek player Dimitri Peters 
In the      Judo World Masters  held in Rabat  Morocco  Darwish came in the third place 
Darwish       s victories during      were also unforgettable  He was the winner of the      Judo Grand Prix  beating Belgium player Toma Nikiforov  The competition was held in Uzbekistan       s capital of Tashkent  from    to    October 
In the      Judo Grand Prix  held in Astana  Kazakhstan from    to    October  he came in third  first place went to the Kazakh Maxim Rakov 
Darwish       s most recent victory was winning the European Judo Open       held in January in the Bulgarian capital of Sofia  in which Darwish won in the final match against Brazil       s Correa Luciano 
Despite being guaranteed a place at Rio       Summer Olympics  Darwish told Daily News Egypt that he will continue to participate in the upcoming  competitions this year  to keep up and improve his performance
