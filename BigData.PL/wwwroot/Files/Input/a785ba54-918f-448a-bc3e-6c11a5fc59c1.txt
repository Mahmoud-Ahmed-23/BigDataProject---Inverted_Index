							El Sewedy Cables said on Tuesday it was negotiating for a    megwatt power generation project in Pakistan  in a bid to open a new market in Asia 
The firm  the largest Arab cable maker by market value  did not provide further details on the project but said Pakistani banks were ready to finance up to    percent of the project 
Pakistani officials said on Sunday that Sewedy was interested in setting up a wind turbine plant in Pakistan and was considering investing      million in five projects of    MW each 
Sewedy has said it is looking into supplying wind energy projects in the Middle East and Africa to tap growing demand 
The firm has production plants in Egypt  Ethiopia  Nigeria  Yemen  Saudi Arabia  Syria  Algeria  Ghana  Zambia  Qatar and Libya and exports to markets in the Middle East North Africa region  Spain  Portugal  eastern Europe and Brazil
